TKDE Poster Track at ICDE 2024 Call for Participation

We are thrilled to invite you to the TKDE Poster Track at ICDE 2024, a collaborative effort between the IEEE International Conference on Data Engineering (ICDE) and the IEEE Transactions on Knowledge and Data Engineering (TKDE).

If your regular research article has been accepted for TKDE between December 1, 2022, and November 30, 2023, you are eligible to present your work in the form of a 2-page extended abstract in the proceedings of ICDE 2024 and a poster at the conference's poster session. ICDE 2024 provides an excellent opportunity to connect with peers, industry leaders, and experts in the field. Make the most out of this opportunity and expand your professional network.

Selection Criteria
The article must align with the ICDE's scope as defined by the ICDE research track and industry track call-for-papers.
The article must NOT be an extension of a paper published in another conference, symposium, or workshop.

Deadlines
All deadlines are 11:59 PM US PST
Submission: December 15, 2023
Notification (Accept/Reject): February 9, 2024
Camera ready: March 1, 2024

Submission Guidelines
The posters should adhere to the ICDE 2024 proceeding formats for submissions. The poster title should be the title of your TKDE paper, followed by ‘(Extended abstract)’. The authors' list should match that of the accepted TKDE paper. The extended abstract should be a summary of the TKDE paper without requiring any additional contributions. Include the TKDE paper's acceptance details and attach an e-copy of your TKDE paper to the abstract submission. The submission should not exceed 2 pages and should be made on the Microsoft CMT https://cmt3.research.microsoft.com/ICDETKDEPosters2024.

Poster Size
Posters should be in the A0 size (84.1 cm × 118.9 cm), in a portrait layout. They can be affixed using double-sided tape.

Registration Policy
The extended abstract will appear in the ICDE 2024 proceedings under a special track for TKDE papers, and the authors must present the work at the ICDE poster session. **Regarding the policy of ICDE registrations, the authors of an extended abstract (TKDE posters) will be treated in the same way as the authors of an ICDE paper.** At least one of the authors must register for the conference before the camera-ready deadline, and this registration can be cancelled only after registration of another author.

We are excited to see your contributions and looking forward to a vibrant exchange of ideas. Feel free to reach out with any questions.
